What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is being able to understand, use and manage your own emotions in a positive light to relive stress, communicate effectively, empathise with others, overcome challenges, succeed at tasks and work, and achieve career/personal goals. It can also be helpful to help connect with your feelings, turn intent to action, and make informed decisions/choices about things that matter.



How is it different to IQ?

IQ (intelligence quotient) a score one can achieve by taking a standardised test which is designed to evaluate intelligence. Whereas EQ (emotional quotient) is the ability to evaluate/control emotions.



Why is it important?

EQ is directly linked with resilience and therefore that may mean - our ability to engage/interact with challenges, maintain/sustain performance, ‘bounce back’ swiftly from adversity, and learn/grow from experiences. With developed EQ, it makes ‘coping with stress’ or ‘stressful conditions’ more manageable and makes it possible to maintain a positive outlook! Chances are that eventually we are likely to burn out at some stage as it is normal to continually push ourselves or be pushed by our seniors/pairs as part of growing. It is therefore important to have sound EQ as a way of dealing with moments of self-doubt or negativity towards ourselves or other around us.
